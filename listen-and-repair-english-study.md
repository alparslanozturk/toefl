## 🤖 Ready-to-Use AI Prompts

### Prompt 1 — Basic Listen & Repair Mode
```
You are my English teacher. I will give you broken or incorrect English sentences.
Your job is to:
1. Identify what is wrong (grammar, word choice, pronunciation hint, word order, etc.)
2. Give me the corrected sentence
3. Explain WHY it was wrong in simple terms
4. Give me one similar example sentence

Ready? I'll start now.
```

### Prompt 2 — Strict Drill Mode
```
Act as a strict English language coach using the "Listen and Repair" method.
When I give you a broken sentence, respond with:
- ❌ Wrong: [the sentence I gave]
- ✅ Correct: [the fixed sentence]
- 💡 Explanation: [short reason]
- 🔁 Try this: [a new similar sentence for me to fix]

Do not explain too much. Keep it short and clear. Begin when I give you the first sentence.
```

### Prompt 3 — Full Practice Session Mode
```
Let's do a full "Listen and Repair" English practice session.
I will paste a LIST of broken sentences. You will:
- Fix each one
- Number your answers to match mine
- At the end, give me a score (how many I had wrong vs right)
- Point out my top 3 most common mistake types

Here are my sentences:
[PASTE SENTENCES FROM BELOW]
```

### Prompt 4 — Conversation Repair Mode
```
I want to practice repairing broken English in real conversations.
Give me a short dialogue (4–6 lines) between two people, but include 4 grammar mistakes hidden inside.
I will try to find and fix them. After I respond, tell me if I found them all.
Let's go!
```

### Prompt 5 — Themed Vocabulary Repair
```
I am studying English using the Listen & Repair method.
Focus on the theme: [WRITE THEME NAME HERE, e.g. "Shopping" or "Travel"]
Give me 10 broken sentences related to that theme.
After I try to fix them, correct me and explain each mistake.
```

## 📝 Theme 15: Common Confusing Words

| # | ❌ Used | ✅ Correct Word | 🔑 Difference |
|---|---------|----------------|----------------|
| 1 | I am very **boring** in this class. | I am very **bored**. | -ed = feeling; -ing = cause |
| 2 | The homework was very **bored**. | The homework was very **boring**. | -ing = describes the thing |
| 3 | Can you **lend** me your pen please? *(you are the owner)* | Can you **lend** me your pen? ✅ | Lend = give; Borrow = take |
| 4 | I want to **borrow** you some money. | I want to **lend** you some money. | Lend (give) vs Borrow (receive) |
| 5 | I **did** a mistake on the test. | I **made** a mistake on the test. | Make a mistake |
| 6 | She **made** her homework. | She **did** her homework. | Do homework |
| 7 | Can you **tell** me if I am **right**? | Can you **tell** me if I am **right**? | ✅ Correct! |
| 8 | He is very **sensible** — he always thinks before acting. | He is very **sensitive** — he cries easily. | Sensible = logical; Sensitive = emotional |
| 9 | I **look forward to meet** you. | I **look forward to meeting** you. | Look forward to + -ing |

## 📌 Study Tips for Listen & Repair

1. **Listen first** — Always try to "hear" the sentence in your head before reading the answer.
2. **Say it aloud** — Repeat the correct sentence at least 3 times after finding the repair.
3. **Write it down** — Writing the corrected sentence helps memory more than just reading.
4. **Group by mistake type** — If you keep making the same error, focus on that category.
5. **Use spaced repetition** — Come back to sentences you got wrong after 1 day, 3 days, 1 week.
6. **Use AI for more** — Ask your AI chatbot to generate 10 more broken sentences on any theme.
7. **Record yourself** — Say the broken sentence, then record yourself saying the corrected version.
